Introduction

The original taxonomy provided a robust starting point by mapping the audio/sound ecosystem across macro categories, industry verticals, economic layers, and regulatory themes. This revision:

· Eliminates the redundant “Audio Applications” layer (its content is now embedded directly into Primary Purpose).
· Sharpens the distinction between ambient sound and commercial background music by defining them through intent (well‑being/masking vs. branding/sales).
· Adds a Conversational & Interactive category to capture voice assistants, IVR, and assistive audio.
· Introduces Live Performance Audio and Sound Design & Foley as explicit creative sub‑branches.
· Converts technology (AI‑Driven, Human‑Crafted, Hybrid) into a horizontal attribute that can be applied to any purpose, eliminating forced hierarchy.
· Enriches every node with current products, services, and business models.

The result is a future‑proof, gap‑free framework for strategists, investors, entrepreneurs, and policymakers.

The Revised Taxonomy

1. Primary Purpose

Why the audio exists – its fundamental intent.

1.1 Creative & Entertainment

Audio designed for artistic expression, storytelling, and entertainment.

1.1.1 AI‑Generated Music & Voice for Entertainment

Products:

· AI Music Generation: AIVA, Amper Music, Soundraw, Boomy, Suno AI
· AI Voice Generation (TTS, voice cloning, real‑time synthesis): ElevenLabs, Murf.ai, Descript, Google WaveNet, Resemble AI, Respeecher, NVIDIA Audio2Face
  Services:
· Bespoke AI‑composed soundtracks, jingles, and sonic branding
· APIs & SDKs for integration (ElevenLabs API, Google Cloud Text‑to‑Speech)
· AI audio strategy consulting
  Business Models: SaaS subscriptions, pay‑per‑use, output licensing, custom project fees.
  Technology: Generative AI, Hybrid (human fine‑tuning).

1.1.2 Human‑Composed & Performed Music & Voice

Products:

· Traditional music production, songwriting, session recording
· Voice acting, narration, character voices
· Live/studio performances captured for distribution
  Services:
· Music licensing (sync, mechanical, performance)
· Sound design studios, voice‑over agencies, session musician hiring
  Business Models: Project fees, royalties, retainer agreements.
  Technology: Human‑Crafted, Passive/Rule‑based.

1.1.3 Sound Design & Foley

Products:

· AI Foley and dynamic sound effects (AI‑powered libraries, procedural audio for games)
· Adaptive game audio (middleware like FMOD, Wwise with AI parameters)
· Spatial audio for VR/AR/MR
  Services:
· Custom sound design for film, TV, games, immersive experiences
· Audio post‑production and mixing
  Business Models: Per‑project billing, licensing packs, integrated platform subscriptions.
  Technology: Generative AI, Hybrid, Human‑Crafted.

1.1.4 Live Performance Audio

Products:

· Live sound reinforcement (PA systems, in‑ear monitors)
· Esports & broadcast audio production
· Immersive concert audio (object‑based mixing, spatial live streams)
  Services:
· Live event audio engineering, front‑of‑house/monitor mixing
· Streaming audio production for live content
  Business Models: Event‑based fees, equipment rental + service packages, long‑term venue contracts.
  Technology: Human‑Crafted, Hybrid (AI‑assisted mixing).

1.2 Functional Enhancement

Audio intended to influence mental state, well‑being, or environmental comfort.

1.2.1 Wellness & Focus

Products:

· Meditation & sleep apps: Calm, Headspace (soundscapes), Relax Melodies
· Focus & productivity audio: Brain.fm, Endel, Focus@Will
· Personalised AI‑generated soundscapes: Endel (AI‑powered), Noisli (custom mixing)
  Services:
· Corporate wellness audio subscriptions
· White‑label solutions for health apps
  Business Models: B2C subscriptions, B2B licensing, in‑app purchases.
  Technology: Generative AI (Endel), Human‑Curated/AI‑assisted, Passive (white noise).

1.2.2 Ambient Masking & Acoustic Comfort

Products:

· Sound masking systems: Soft dB, Cambridge Sound Management, Lencore
· Office/healthcare acoustic comfort solutions: dynamic noise control, HVAC‑integrated masking
· Nature & white noise generators: myNoise, dedicated hardware devices
  Services:
· Workplace acoustic consulting, installation, and tuning
· IoT‑connected masking networks
  Business Models: Hardware sales + SaaS management, B2B service contracts, per‑square‑metre pricing.
  Technology: Passive/Rule‑based, Analytical AI (adaptive masking).

1.2.3 Environmental Soundscapes

Products:

· Theme park & museum immersive audio installations
· Smart city informational sound beacons, directional speakers
· Art installations & experiential environments
  Services:
· Bespoke soundscape design, hardware integration, content management
· Ongoing content refresh services
  Business Models: Project‑based design fees, hardware leasing, recurring content subscription.
  Technology: Hybrid, Human‑Crafted, Generative AI.

---

1.3 Commercial Experience

Audio deployed to enhance brand perception, influence customer behaviour, and drive sales.

1.3.1 Background Music & Curated Playlists

Products:

· Licensed streaming for business: Mood Media, Soundtrack Your Brand, Cloud Cover Music, Rockbot
· Curated playlists by genre/mood/time of day
· Automated scheduling and remote management dashboards
  Services:
· Music curation and profiling for retail, hospitality, fitness
· Licensing & compliance management (PRS/PPL/BMI/ASCAP coverage)
· Customer analytics integration
  Business Models: Monthly per‑location subscriptions, tiered plans by features, hardware + SaaS bundles.
  Technology: Human‑Curated, AI‑Assisted curation (Hybrid).

1.3.2 Sonic Branding & Audio Identity

Products:

· Sonic logos, brand mnemonics, audio signatures (e.g., Mastercard melody)
· Branded voice personas (synthetic voices for brand consistency)
· Custom AI‑generated jingles and brand soundtracks
  Services:
· Agency‑led sonic strategy and identity creation
· Audio style guides and asset libraries
· Voice cloning for brand spokespersons
  Business Models: Project fees, retainer partnerships, licensing of branded audio assets.
  Technology: Human‑Crafted, Generative AI, Hybrid.

1.3.3 Adaptive In‑Store Audio

Products:

· Real‑time adaptive systems: Audoo (environment‑responsive), NSM Music
· AI that changes music based on demographics, dwell time, weather
· Programmatic audio advertising in‑store
  Services:
· Behaviour analytics and A/B testing for audio
· Integration with POS, footfall sensors, CRM
  Business Models: SaaS + data analytics fees, hardware leasing, pay‑per‑performance uplift.
  Technology: Analytical AI, Generative AI, Hybrid.

1.4 Conversational & Interactive

Audio that enables two‑way interaction, information retrieval, navigation, or accessibility.

1.4.1 Voice Assistants & Chatbots

Products:

· Consumer: Amazon Alexa, Google Assistant, Apple Siri (voice output)
· Enterprise: custom IVA platforms (Nuance, Kore.ai, Rasa)
· In‑car voice interaction: Cerence, SoundHound
  Services:
· Voice‑user‑interface (VUI) design, persona development
· TTS integration and wake‑word customisation
  Business Models: Platform licensing, per‑request pricing, enterprise SaaS.
  Technology: Generative AI (TTS, NLG), Analytical AI (NLU), Hybrid.

1.4.2 IVR & Customer Service Audio

Products:

· Cloud‑based IVR with natural TTS: Twilio, Amazon Connect, Google Dialogflow CX
· On‑hold messaging and queue‑management systems
· Sentiment‑adaptive voice prompts
  Services:
· Professional voice recording, multilingual TTS optimisation
· Conversational design consulting
  Business Models: Per‑minute/usage fees, monthly platform subscriptions.
  Technology: Generative AI (TTS), Human‑Crafted (pre‑recorded), Hybrid.

1.4.3 Navigation & Way‑finding Audio

Products:

· Public transport announcements (dynamic TTS)
· Indoor navigation audio beacons (Bluetooth/AoA)
· Museum audio guides with location triggers
  Services:
· Multi‑language audio content production
· Hardware installation and software management
  Business Models: System integration fees, content licensing, hardware + SaaS.
  Technology: Generative AI, Passive/Pre‑recorded.

1.4.4 Accessibility & Assistive Audio

Products:

· Screen readers (JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver) and text‑to‑speech engines
· Audio description for video (AI‑generated and human‑narrated)
· Hearing loop systems, assistive listening devices (ALS)
· Sound‑based navigation for visually impaired (e.g., Microsoft Soundscape)
  Services:
· Accessibility compliance audits, integration with web/app content
· Bespoke audio description production
  Business Models: Software licences, project fees, government/institutional contracts.
  Technology: Generative AI, Human‑Crafted, Passive.

2. Technology Attribute (Cross‑cutting)

Apply any of these tags to the products/services above to define how the audio is created or processed. This replaces the separate “By Technology” branch, enabling multi‑dimensional analysis.

· Generative AI – AI creates original music, voice, or sound from scratch (e.g., Suno AI, ElevenLabs).
· Analytical AI – AI analyses environment, behaviour, or content to adapt audio in real time (e.g., Audoo adaptive playlists, sentiment‑based IVR).
· Human‑Crafted – Entirely created, performed, or curated by humans (e.g., live orchestra, traditional voice‑over).
· Hybrid – AI‑assisted human creativity or human‑supervised AI output (e.g., AI‑generated jingle finalised by a composer, curated playlists with AI recommendations).
· Passive / Rule‑based – Simple loops, white noise, pre‑recorded files with no intelligent generation (e.g., fan‑noise apps, basic masking systems).

5. Governance & Ethics

Cross‑cutting regulatory and ethical considerations. Map each theme to the relevant Primary Purpose to assess risk.

· Intellectual Property & Copyright
  · Ownership of AI‑generated music/voice; voice cloning rights (particularly affects Creative & Entertainment, Commercial Experience, Conversational).
  · Licensing clarity for adaptive audio systems.
· Privacy & Data Protection
  · Audio recording in public/private spaces (Ambient Masking, Adaptive In‑Store Audio, Voice Assistants).
  · Consent for behavioural analytics via sound (GDPR, CCPA).
· Accessibility Standards
  · WCAG, ADA compliance for audio description, screen readers, hearing augmentation (Conversational & Interactive, all digital products).
  · Inclusive design of voice interfaces.
· Transparency & Disclosure
  · Labelling AI‑generated voices and deepfake detection (Creative, Conversational).
  · Consumer notification when adaptive audio is analysing behaviour.
· Environmental & Social Impact
  · Noise pollution from public‑space audio; cultural sensitivity of background music; energy footprint of generative models.

How to Use This Taxonomy

For Strategists & Market Analysts

· Identify growth sectors by combining Primary Purpose with Technology Attribute (e.g., Generative AI in Wellness & Focus is still nascent but accelerating).
· Map competitive landscapes by placing existing companies within the hierarchy and noting empty nodes (e.g., few pure‑play “Environmental Soundscapes” providers using analytical AI).

For Investors

· Evaluate TAM by sizing Industry Verticals crossed with purpose (e.g., total addressable spend on Background Music in retail).
· Assess technology risk by checking Governance & Ethics tags; an AI voice cloning startup will face significant IP and transparency headwinds.

For Entrepreneurs & Product Managers

· Uncover product gaps: the intersection of “Accessibility & Assistive Audio” and “Generative AI” (e.g., on‑the‑fly AI audio description) is underserved.
· Design business models by referencing the Economic Layers for analogous structures (e.g., many “Wellness & Focus” apps succeed with B2C subscriptions and B2B licensing).

For Policymakers & Compliance Officers

· Use the Governance & Ethics section as a checklist for any audio deployment, ensuring that privacy, accessibility, and IP obligations are met.
· The Technology Attribute flags high‑risk areas: any node tagged “Analytical AI” demands a privacy impact assessment.
